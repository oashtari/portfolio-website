Career

My career has been quite a journey.

Years 0-8

I worked at two different talent agencies, the first one for less than a year. I delivered mail, coffee, fruit, flowers, but eventually became a TV agent representing writers, directors, producers and other TV talent.

Years 8-11

Turns out, I didn't love being an agent, so I had the opportunity to become a digital agent as the world of YouTube stars was in its nascent stages. Still didn't love it, so then there was a year long "what's next" expedition.

Years 11-14

I joined a social platform, fortuitously for me, they needed someone in LA to spearhead talent relationships. That turned into not just TV talent, but also sports, music and other entertainers.

Years 14-16

My curiosity had grown and working with talent was no longer of interest, but I was fortunate to get an opportunity to learn, tackle and scale business operations with the same group of colleagues, quite a learning period.

Years 16-20

Utilizing that operations knowledge, I joined a small start-up focusing all my time on bringing efficiency to the business and helping keep the doors open.
